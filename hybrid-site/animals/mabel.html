Mabel believes puddles are personal insults. After last night's downpour she surveyed the barnyard with the severity of a runway coach. Every low spot gleamed. Every hoof print filled. She snorted, squared her shoulders, and began rehearsing her escape choreography.

Attempt one: tiptoeing over the shallows with exaggerated high steps. Attempt two: convincing Chester the rooster to test the depth ("for science"). Attempt three: a dramatic launch that landed squarely in the middle, splattering mud up to her eyelashes.

She held still, blinking through the droplets, then announced to the assembled donkeys, "It's called rustic couture." She swished her tail like a gown, strutted past the crowd, and demanded a towel. Argus offered his flank instead. She accepted with queenly grace.
