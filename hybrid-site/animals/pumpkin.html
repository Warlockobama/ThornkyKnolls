Pumpkin greets sunrise like a foreman checking a blueprint. She plants her square hooves at the hill crest, surveys the entire pasture, and only then permits the rest of the herd to spill out of the barn. If anyone doubts her authority, she simply flicks her fringe and the conversation ends.

Her first stop is the galvanized tub where the night breeze has painted a thin rind of ice. One tap with those crescent horns snaps it clean. Then she paces the lower fence line, counting donkeys, mules, and chickens with the patience of a librarian. Each resident gets a polite nudge until they settle into their chore.

Pumpkin is equal parts charm and order. She will let a calf nap on her shoulder, but she will not tolerate a crooked mineral block. Gates swing shut behind her. Stray buckets find their way home. By the time the sun clears the hay loft, the ranch feels buttoned and ready.

The best part? She waits until everyone else eats before she touches her own ration. She claims it keeps morale tidy. We suspect she just likes hearing all the grateful chewing first.
