Our story

A family ranch, a family name

Thorny Knolls is a family operation in the Shenandoah foothills, built on hard work, humor, and a deep love of animals.

The name behind the name

The name "Thorny" is a tribute to our late grandfather, Michael "Thorny" Thistle, and our grandmother, Bonnie Thistle. Thorny was a hard-working, blue-collar man who did all kinds of jobs — from owning a bait shop to plumbing, where he retired as a proud member of the plumbers union. We wish he was here to share our beautiful ranch, but we keep his spirit alive through hard work, humor, and a love of animals.

A natural bond

Bonnie was a kind soul who had a special bond with animals. As a child, she made the local paper for taming a squirrel — and that gentle, patient way with animals runs through everything we do at Thorny Knolls. It's in the way we handle our cattle daily, how we raise every calf with care, and why we believe good temperament starts with good people.

What we do

We're a small family ranch focused on producing excellent Highland genetics. Our fold is raised on grass in Warrenton, Virginia, handled daily, and bred with intention. We focus on desirable traits — classic coats, solid conformation, and calm temperament — so that every calf that leaves here is something we're proud of.
