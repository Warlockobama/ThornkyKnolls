The Great Feeding Frenzy

Featuring: Argus, Willow, and Sega

Dinner time at Thorny Knolls is never just about food - it's a full theatrical production, and tonight's performance featured our equine trio in all their glory.

Argus, our dignified standard donkey, was the first to notice me heading toward the barn with feed buckets. His ears perked up, and he let out a single, deep bray - his subtle way of saying "I see you, and I expect my dinner to be prompt." He made his way to the feeding area with measured steps, as if rushing would be beneath his dignity.

Willow, our clever mule, had already figured out that I'd be approaching from the north gate today. I found her waiting there, having somehow unlatched the gate between pastures to position herself optimally. She gave me a look that clearly said "What took you so long?" Her intelligence is both impressive and occasionally inconvenient when it comes to farm security.

Then there was Sega, our gentle giant. The massive mammoth donkey approached with his characteristic slow, deliberate gait, each footfall shaking the ground slightly. Despite his imposing size, he waited patiently for the smaller animals to get their portions first, content to loom protectively nearby until everyone else was settled.

As I distributed the evening meal, the dynamics were fascinating to watch. Argus ate with refined precision, every bite measured and deliberate. Willow, having secured her spot through strategic positioning, ate quickly and efficiently - likely already planning tomorrow's gate-opening escapade. And Sega, once everyone else was served, finally lowered his great head to his feed bucket, eating with surprising delicacy for such a large creature.

The most amusing moment came when a gust of wind blew a flake of hay toward Willow's bucket. Both she and Argus moved for it simultaneously, resulting in a brief standoff. Willow won through sheer persistence, while Argus withdrew with a snort that seemed to say "I didn't want that hay anyway."

As the sun set and dinner concluded, the three settled into their evening routine. Argus returned to his observation post by the fence, Willow tested a few more latches "just to keep her skills sharp," and Sega stood watch over the farm, a patient guardian against the approaching night.
