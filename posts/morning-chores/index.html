Morning Chores at Thorny Knolls

Featuring: Pumpkin, Lebella, and Dobby

The first rays of sunlight crept over the horizon, painting the sky in shades of pink and gold. At Thorny Knolls, mornings have a rhythm all their own, and today was no exception.

Pumpkin was already awake, standing beneath her favorite oak tree with her shaggy coat catching the early light. She watched patiently as I approached with the morning feed, her big eyes tracking my every movement. Unlike some of the more enthusiastic greeters on the farm, Pumpkin prefers to wait with dignified patience, knowing her breakfast will arrive in due time.

Lebella, ever the matriarch, was already organizing the herd. She positioned herself near the feeding area, gently nudging the younger cows into a more orderly arrangement. Her leadership is subtle but effective - a look here, a soft moo there, and suddenly everyone knows exactly where they should be.

Then came Dobby, our miniature donkey alarm system. His braying announcement of my arrival could probably be heard three farms over. Despite his small size, Dobby believes it's his solemn duty to inform everyone - human and animal alike - about every single event occurring on the property. This morning's event? The arrival of carrots.

As I distributed the morning feed, the three of them fell into their usual routine. Pumpkin ate slowly and methodically, savoring each bite. Lebella kept one eye on her food and one eye on the rest of the herd, still in mom-mode even during breakfast. And Dobby? He finished his portion in record time and then attempted to convince everyone else that he hadn't been fed yet, his big eyes pleading with practiced innocence.

By the time the sun had fully risen, morning chores were complete, and peace had settled over Thorny Knolls once again. Well, relative peace - Dobby was already planning his next dramatic announcement.
