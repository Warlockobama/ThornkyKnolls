Origin story

Field notes from the understory

How a wind-leaned hillside, a few shaggy cows, and stubborn fences grew into a slow, careful herd.

Started as an experiment

Thorny Knolls began as a patch that refused to stay tidy: brambles, spruce, and a wind that leaned the same way for a decade. The first Highland stepped onto the grass more as an experiment than a business plan.

Built by repetition

Fences must hold, water must run. Animals must be fed, hooves must trim, and gates must close. The work is honest, and the design follows suit: hard borders, obvious structure, and room to breathe.

Stewardship first

Low-volume sales keep the herd measured. Hands stay on horns and halters to keep them gentle. Visitors learn to move slow enough to see ears swivel, tails flick, and breath hang in cold air.
