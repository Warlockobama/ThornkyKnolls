First Calf, Fast Learner

Anabella Maps the Meadow

Every day Anabella chooses a new landmark to conquer. This week it was the tall clover beside the rain gauge. She circled the post twenty-one times before daring to touch it with her nose. When she finally did, she leapt backward, tossed her fuzzy bangs, and fired off a victory lap worthy of a county fair.

She is all soft edges and sudden curiosity. One minute she is shadowing Lebella's stride, the next minute she's balancing on a small mound of river rock, practicing her “big cow stance.” Any bucket left unattended becomes a drum. Any puddle becomes a mirror. The donkey crew has adopted her as their honorary mascot.

Training sessions are short and sweet. She wears a halter for exactly four minutes before wriggling free, then returns on her own with an expression that says, “Again, but with more cookies.” Patience is her superpower; enthusiasm is ours to keep up.

Field Notes

  • Snack preference: Crushed peppermint diluted with warm milk
  • Nap partner: Pumpkin whenever she can sneak beneath that wooly chin
  • Curiosity rating: Off the chart — locks must be double checked
  • Training reward: Scritches along the jawline, never skip the ears
  • Favorite sound: The click of the pasture gate opening
~ Jotted by the apprentice wrangler, still out of breath
