Pocket Inspector General

Dobby's Great Mitten Caper

Dobby has developed a new morning routine: pocket inventory. He greets each volunteer with a polite sniff, then slides his soft nose into every coat pocket until he finds something worthy of attention. Today it was a pair of knit mittens dangling temptingly from a sleeve.

He tugged once, twice, then bolted across the paddock with the mitten waving like a victory flag. Willow called after him; Argus pretended not to laugh. Dobby zigzagged between hay bales until we bribed him with a peppermint. He surrendered the mitten only after we promised an extra minute of ear scritches.

Later he returned the second mitten on his own, carefully placing it on the fence post nearest the tack room. Mischief complete, he curled up beside the salt lick and hummed himself to sleep.

Field Notes

  • Treasure preferences: Wool mittens, shoelaces, zipper pulls
  • Safest bribe: Peppermint halves warmed in your palm
  • Power move: Standing on hind legs to request hugs
  • Favorite phrase: “House elf privilege!” (we swear he understands)
  • Recharge routine: Naps under the mineral block canopy
~ Documented by the mitten recovery squad
