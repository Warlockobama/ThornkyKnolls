Feeding Frenzy Protocol

Chaos choreographed, mostly

Lunch hour at Thorny Knolls is a ballet performed in barn boots. The donkeys form an eager semicircle, the Highlands line up by seniority, and the horses pretend they are above the fray — until the first carrot hits the bucket.

We deploy a three-bucket system: one for mules, one for cows, one for the opportunists (looking at you, Dobby). Willow whistles twice to signal distribution. Gus tries to cut the line, gets redirected by Pumpkin’s slow blink, and accepts his fate with theatrical sighs.

Halfway through, Mabel insists her carrot coins be dried with a towel. Argus stations himself between the mules to enforce personal space. Hugo nibbles politely from the far side, happy to be included, happy to stay clear of the scrum.

Ten minutes later the buckets ring hollow, the ground is spotless, and every muzzle glistens with carrot juice. Not a single ration lost, only a few egos mussed. Success.

Checklist

  • Carrots: 18 whole, sliced into coins
  • Supplement: 2 scoops of mineral crumble for the senior crew
  • Rules: No shoving, no double dipping, no unsanctioned singing
  • Cleanup: Rinse buckets, hang upside down, sweep hoof prints
  • Reward: 15-minute nap window before afternoon chores
~ Documented by the noon mess hall captain
