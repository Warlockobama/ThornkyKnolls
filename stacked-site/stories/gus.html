Breakfast Club Forever

Gus and the Bucket Coup

This morning Gus announced a “strategic realignment” of the feeding lineup. Translation: he liberated the apple bucket and marched it to the far oak tree where the sun catches the dew like glitter. The donkeys trailed behind him chanting encouragement.

He parked the bucket atop a perfectly flat limestone slab, tapped it twice with his horn, and refused to begin until we produced a ceremonial apple slice. Once tribute was paid, he rationed treats with the seriousness of a banker. One slice for Mabel (with flair), one for Willow (with instructions), one for himself (with a wink).

When the chore bell rang, he nudged the bucket back toward the barn, letting it clang rhythmically against the packed earth. The whole procession felt like a parade started on a dare. By the time the bucket reached its hook, every animal had eaten, and Gus had declared the morning a triumph of “participatory breakfast governance.”

Field Notes

  • Absolute requirement: One red apple slice resting on top of the grain
  • Secret project: Engineering a counterweight for the squeaky gate
  • Nemesis: Plastic bags that flutter near the compost heap
  • Best friend: Anyone who scratches between his horns exactly seven times
  • Philosophy: “Order breakfast, then order the day.”
~ Submitted by the grain steward, still laughing
