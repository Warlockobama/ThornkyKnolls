Switchback Student

Hugo Finds His Pace

Hugo came to us with a cautious heart and a generous stride. Today we introduced him to the hillside switchback. He sniffed every footprint first, then planted his hooves and followed our lead rope one deliberate step at a time.

Halfway up he paused to stare across the valley, ears swiveling toward the distant creek. A flock of geese startled from the water and he trembled, but instead of bolting he leaned into the pressure of the halter, took a deep breath, and kept climbing. We cheered. He flapped his lips in surprise, then picked up a brave little jog.

At the summit he received a pocketful of carrot coins and a long stretch of scratches along his withers. He pressed his forehead against our shoulders and exhaled a sigh that felt like trust beginning to bloom.

Field Notes

  • Fuel: Thin-cut carrot coins dipped in molasses water
  • Motivation: A steady voice humming trail songs
  • Comfort item: Wool saddle pad warmed in the sun
  • Training mantra: “Small steps, big views.”
  • Aftercare: Hoof pick, leg wrap, deep hay nap
~ Noted by the trail crew, boots muddy and hearts full
