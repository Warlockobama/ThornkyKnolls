Matriarch in Motion

Lebella's Storm Circuit

When the Ozark sky darkens, Lebella shifts into architect mode. She glides along the ridge line, brushing her horns against the woven wire to feel for any slack. The calves fall in behind her like apprentices tracing a blueprint.

She pauses at each post, leaning against it with a purposeful shoulder. If the post wobbles, she stamps twice — a signal for us to bring the post driver. If it holds, she nods and moves on. The donkeys know better than to interrupt the inspection; even Gus waits quietly by the feed shed until the safety tour receives a nod.

By the time rain begins to prick the dust, Lebella has steered everyone toward the cedar windbreak. She stands at the opening, letting each animal brush past her ribs, murmuring low encouragement until the last tail disappears inside. Only then does she claim the corner that faces the weather, eyes half closed, guard fully awake.

Field Notes

  • Preferred treat: Molasses cubes kept in the pocket closest to your heartbeat
  • Bridle size: None required — she follows hand signals and eyebrow lifts
  • Rain protocol: First inside, last asleep
  • Sound she loves: Wind chimes on the equipment shed
  • Sacred ritual: A brisk forehead rub before dusk rounds
~ Recorded by the fence team on storm watch
