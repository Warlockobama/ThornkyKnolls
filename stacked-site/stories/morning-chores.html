Morning Chores Roll Call

Fog, feathers, and fresh hay

Chester sounded reveille exactly at 4:47 AM. The barn sighed, the donkeys yawned, and Pumpkin cracked the silence with a low hum. By 5:12 the doors swung open and the first ribbon of sunrise cut through the cedar smoke from our coffee pot.

We split tasks with military precision. One crew flaked hay while another refilled the mineral buffet. Willow performed a headcount, Dobby checked everyone's pockets, and Argus supervised from his shade post even though the sun hadn't cleared the ridge.

When the grain cart squeaked, Gus appeared with his bucket manifesto, lobbying for apple slices before oats. We negotiated half a slice upfront and half as dessert. Peace restored, the herd settled into their breakfast cadence, punctuated by soft chomps and the occasional delighted snort.

By 6:03 the pads were swept, the tack hooks polished, and the pasture gates opened one by one. The fog lifted to reveal a chorus line of happy, hay-dusted faces ready to meet the day.
