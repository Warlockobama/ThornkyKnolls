Foundation Horse

Raider's Twilight Rounds

Raider earned his gray hairs building this ranch, and he will tell you about each of them. While the younger herd settles, he insists on one slow lap of the perimeter at sunset. His hooves fall into the same rhythm they've held since the first fence post was set.

Tonight he paused at the gate overlooking the lower pasture. Smoke ambled up beside him and the two old friends touched noses, trading stories we could almost hear. Then Raider swung south, checking the latch on the tool shed and the water level in the trough before nudging the lantern hook with his muzzle. Tradition satisfied, he allowed himself to wander back to the barn.

We slipped him an extra scoop of senior grain and brushed out the dust halos around his eyes. He leaned into the curry comb, sighing the way only a horse with a lifetime of miles can. When the fireflies blinked awake, Raider flicked his ears toward them and nodded as if answering role call.

Field Notes

  • Favorite memory: Breaking trail for the very first hay delivery
  • Health ritual: Warm mash with turmeric and a dash of cinnamon
  • Evening treat: Two thin apple slices served in sequence
  • Trusted companion: Smoke, keeper of the barn lore
  • Signature sound: Soft whicker that means “lights out, friends.”
~ Logged by the founders, lanterns in hand
