First Snow

Annabel, Ness, Pumpkin

It started around four in the morning — light, quiet, the kind that doesn't register until dawn breaks and the whole pasture has gone white. Annabel was the first to notice. She stood at the edge of the loafing shed, stretched her neck out, and caught a flake on her nose. Then she bolted.

Full speed across the front field, kicking up powder that hadn't been there an hour ago. Ness watched from Pumpkin's side, unsure whether this was fun or an emergency. By his third lap around the hay ring he'd decided it was fun. Pumpkin didn't move. She stood at her usual post, snow collecting on her fringe, blinking slowly like she'd seen this before and would see it again.

Lebella brought Adele out around seven, both of them stepping carefully through the fresh layer. We double-checked the trough heater and threw an extra bale into the shed. By noon the snow had stopped, the calves were tired, and the whole herd was packed into the loafing shed shoulder to shoulder, steam rising off their backs like a small highland sauna.

Checklist

  • Snowfall: ~3 inches, light and dry
  • Trough heater: confirmed working, no ice
  • Extra hay: 1 bale broken into loafing shed
  • Calf energy level: unreasonable
  • Pumpkin's reaction: dignified indifference
