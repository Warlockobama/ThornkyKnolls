HHH LaBella

Registration #63503 · Fullblood Highland

LaBella is a dun, horned, fullblood Highland cow born April 2, 2022, registered under herd ID AEH14L. She was bred by Emily, Eric, and Fritz Young. Her sire is Skye High Cornerstone (D)+, tracing back through LEA Haaken (D) and LEA Forbes (D) on the top line, and through CH Aloysius (D) and Skye High Vermillion (PV,D) to Gilchrist's Angela (D) on the bottom. Her dam is Sweetbrier Naug Bella (D), descending from Sunset Yardley and Sunset Limited Edition (CA,AI,D) on the top side, and through Sweetbrier Naugee and Sunset Kaelen to Sunset Heavenly Lass on the bottom.

Lebella carries a soft dun coat — sandy gold with darker points along the ears and muzzle — and a thick, even fringe that sweeps just past her eyes. She has solid width through the chest, a balanced frame, and a calm, level topline. Her horns are well set with a clean, wide sweep. She is an easy keeper on grass and has produced two quality fullblood calves by AF Nonsuch Trillium (D), both showing her steady temperament and correct conformation.

She is handled daily, halter-trained, and quiet for all routine management. Her disposition is calm and maternal — attentive to her calves without being reactive — which makes her an excellent brood cow and a pleasure to work around. She would suit any program looking for proven dun genetics with solid Highland type.

Lebella is the quietest cow in the pasture and somehow the one everybody listens to. She doesn't call, doesn't push — she just starts walking and the calves fall in. If she moves toward the barn, they move toward the barn. If she decides it's time to graze the back field, that's where you'll find the whole group twenty minutes later.

She's the best mother we've got. Her calves come out confident because she lets them explore but never lets them get too far. She'll watch Anabella tear across the paddock at full speed without flinching, then give one low rumble when it's time to come back. Works every time. She's calm enough to stand for anything — hoof trim, vet visit, new halter — and that calm is what she passes on.
