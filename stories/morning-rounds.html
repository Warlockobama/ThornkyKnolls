Morning Rounds

Pumpkin, Lebella, Adele

Pumpkin was already at the gate when we came down the hill. She does that — stations herself at the first latch point and waits like she punched in early. Lebella was thirty yards back with Adele tucked against her flank, both of them steaming in the cold air.

We checked the water trough first. Thin ice along the edges, but the heater held overnight. Pumpkin drank long and slow while Lebella nudged Adele toward the hay rack. By the time we finished topping off the mineral feeder, the whole group had sorted itself into the usual order — Pumpkin at the front, Lebella watching from the middle, calves tucked between.

No limps, no coughs, no fence gaps. A clean start to the day. We scattered a few extra flakes of hay along the east fence line to draw them toward the windbreak before the next cold front moves in tonight.

Checklist

  • Mineral feeder: topped off with loose mineral
  • Hay: 4 flakes east fence, 3 flakes loafing shed
